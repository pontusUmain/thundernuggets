# Dahl App — Prototype Workshop Brief

**Prepared by umain | March 2026 | Confidential**

---

## A. Background & Purpose

Dahl is Sweden's leading wholesale supplier for plumbing, heating, and ventilation (VVS), with SEK 8B in annual revenue, 75 stores, and 38,000 craftspeople served. Despite this dominant market position, Dahl faces a structural challenge: its digital experience is fragmented. Installers currently split their workflow across two separate apps — one for ordering (Dahl) and one for job management (Flexibla Kontoret) — alongside manual steps for quoting, invoicing, and time reporting.

The broader industry context compounds this: only 20% of building materials companies have fully adopted digital commerce. Competitors like Ahlsell are investing heavily, and the window for first-mover advantage is closing.

The purpose of this workshop is twofold. First, to **validate a set of hypotheses** about what a unified installer experience could look like if it was built around the actual rhythm of a working day. Second, to **produce something tangible for the client** — a working prototype that demonstrates the core experience and sparks a concrete conversation about direction, scope, and next steps.

---

## B. Target Groups

**The Field Installer** A VVS tradesperson spending 80%+ of their day on job sites — basements, crawl spaces, new builds — often without reliable signal. They own or work for a small installation firm, are already Dahl customers, and want tools that reduce friction without adding admin. Their phone is their office.

**The Installation Company Manager** Runs a small-to-medium VVS firm with a team of installers. Cares about project margins, team oversight, and keeping purchasing consolidated. Already uses Flexibla Kontoret and sees real value in tighter integration between job management and ordering.

---

## C. Suggested Scope

The prototype should demonstrate four core sections of the app, each mapped to a distinct part of the installer's workflow.

**Home** A personal dashboard that gives the installer an at-a-glance summary of their day — active projects, pending tasks, recent orders, and any alerts. The entry point that orients everything else.

**My Day** A task and schedule planner built around the working day. What jobs are on today, where, and in what order. The goal is to give installers a structured view of their day before they leave in the morning and a way to stay on top of it while in the field.

**E-Commerce** Dahl's product catalog, ordering, and reorder flows — unified and optimized for mobile use on the job site. Includes barcode scanning and AI-powered visual product search.

**Work Assistant** The intelligent layer of the app. Project notes, AI-powered problem solving, product recommendations in context, time tracking, and cost allocation per project. This is where the app goes beyond commerce and becomes a genuine work tool.

## D. Hypotheses

The following hypotheses frame what the prototype should explore and what the team should aim to validate.

**H1 — The app can be designed around a working day** An installer's needs follow a clear rhythm: preparing before leaving, executing on site, and wrapping up after. An app structured around this arc — morning briefing, in-field support, end-of-day summary — will feel intuitive in a way that generic B2B tools never do.

**H2 — We can help installers solve problems, not just find products** The real need isn't always "find this SKU." It's "this fitting doesn't match, what do I do?" An AI-powered work assistant that understands VVS context can answer that — and surface the right product as part of the answer, not instead of it.

**H3 — We can help users keep track of cost per project in real time** Installers rarely know their margin until the job is done. By automatically tagging every order, material, and hour to a project, the app can give a live cost-vs-budget view that removes end-of-month surprises.

**H4 — We can help users keep track of and plan their day** Between multiple job sites, supply runs, and ad-hoc tasks, the working day is hard to manage. A lightweight planner built into the app — connected to their projects and orders — can bring structure without adding admin burden.

**H5 — Routine tasks like time tracking can be automated** Time reporting is universally disliked and inconsistently done. Using location, calendar, and job data, the app can infer when an installer arrives and leaves a site, suggest time entries automatically, and reduce manual logging to a simple confirmation tap.
